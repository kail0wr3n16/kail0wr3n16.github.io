Privacy Policy for Verbum

Last updated: January 18, 2026

Overview

Verbum is a Latin to English dictionary app. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about how our app works.

Information We Collect

Camera Access

Verbum accesses your device's camera to scan and recognize Latin text. All text recognition is performed locally on your device using Google ML Kit. No images or photos are uploaded to external servers. Camera data is processed entirely on-device and is not stored.

Dictionary Lookups

When you look up a Latin word, the word you searched is sent to our dictionary service to retrieve the definition. We do not log or store your search history on our servers.

Information We Do Not Collect

  • Personal information (name, email, etc.)
  • Location data
  • Device identifiers
  • Usage analytics
  • Photos or images from your camera

Third-Party Services

Google ML Kit: We use Google ML Kit for on-device text recognition. This processing happens entirely on your device. For more information, see Google ML Kit Terms.

Data Storage

Verbum may store your preferences and recent searches locally on your device. This data is not transmitted to us or any third party.

Children's Privacy

Verbum does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The app is suitable for users of all ages as it does not collect any personal data.

Data Security

Since we do not collect or store personal data on external servers, there is minimal risk to your privacy. All sensitive processing (text recognition) occurs locally on your device.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date.
